# Startup GTM Strategy

A comprehensive skill for building Go-To-Market strategies for early-stage startups, synthesized from frameworks by MRR Unlocked, GTM Strategist (Maja Voje), Posthog, and April Dunford's positioning methodology.

## Overview

This skill guides founders through a complete GTM workflow:
1. Start with Early Customer Profile (ECP) - who to target first
2. Progress to Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) - who to target at scale
3. Develop positioning using the competitive alternatives framework
4. Validate with market signals

---

## Phase 1: Early Customer Profile (ECP)

**Key insight:** Before finding your ideal customer, you must win your early customers. ECP is who you target first to build evidence.

### ECP Selection Criteria

Your ECP must satisfy ALL four criteria:

| Criterion | Description | Why It Matters |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| **Burning Pain** | Problem needs solving NOW | Short sales cycles, high urgency |
| **Willingness to Pay** | Has budget and authority | Early revenue validation |
| **Your Proximity** | You can win them in 3 months | Realistic acquisition |
| **Relevant Case Studies** | Use cases apply to future ICP | Builds credible social proof |

### 5 ECP Archetypes

From analysis of 400+ launches, these are the customer types most likely to be early adopters:

1. **Technology Evangelist** - Early adopter mindset, high risk tolerance, active in niche communities
2. **Underdog Supporter** - Small teams trying to compete, budget-conscious but decisive
3. **Game Changer** - Innovation-focused decision makers, looking for competitive advantage
4. **Digital Transformer** - Organizations undergoing change, mandate to modernize
5. **Agent of Change** - Internal champions driving adoption, personal stake in success

### ECP Template

```
EARLY CUSTOMER PROFILE
======================
Name/Persona: [e.g., "Founder Tim"]

DEMOGRAPHICS
- Company size: [e.g., 5-50 employees]
- Industry: [specific vertical]
- Stage: [e.g., seed, early growth]

BEHAVIORAL TRAITS
- Burning pain: [specific problem they face NOW]
- Current solution: [what they do today]
- Decision speed: [how fast they can buy]
- Risk tolerance: [high/medium/low]

CONTEXT
- Where they hang out: [communities, events, platforms]
- Who influences them: [thought leaders, peers]
- Buying triggers: [what events prompt purchase]
```

### ECP → ICP Progression

```
Early Users → Early Customers → Ideal Customers
   (free)        (paid)           (high ACV, low churn)
   
Timeline:       0-6 months      6-18 months      18+ months
```

## Phase 2: Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### ICP Framework Structure

**PART A: Ideal Company**
- Company Size (employees, revenue range)
- Company Stage (startup, growth, enterprise)
- Industry/Vertical
- Tech Stack (tools they use)
- Growth Rate & Funding Stage

**PART B: User Persona**
- Job Title (specific, not generic)
- Department & Team Size
- Daily Activities & Responsibilities
- KPIs/Success Metrics
- Pain Points & Jobs to be Done
- Content Consumed & Communities

**PART C: Buyer Persona**
- Job Title (often more senior)
- Priorities & Decision Criteria
- Budget Authority
- Stakeholders Involved
- Risk Tolerance

**PART D: Trigger Events**

| Trigger Type | Examples |
|--------------|----------|
| Company Events | Funding raised, new exec hired, expansion |
| Technology | Legacy system sunset, compliance deadline |
| Personnel | New VP hired, team expansion |
| Business | Revenue targets missed, growth mandate |

### Anti-ICP

Define who NOT to target:
- Long sales cycles with low win rates
- Low contract values relative to effort
- High churn / support volume
- Don't value your differentiation

## Phase 3: Positioning Framework

Based on April Dunford's methodology. **Follow this sequence in order.**

### Positioning Flow

```
1. COMPETITIVE ALTERNATIVES
   "What would customer do if we didn't exist?"
   → Include "do nothing" / status quo
   
2. DIFFERENTIATED FEATURES
   "What do we have that alternatives don't?"
   
3. VALUE FOR CUSTOMERS
   "So what? What's the benefit?"
   
4. CUSTOMERS WHO CARE
   "Who cares A LOT about this value?"
   
5. MARKET CATEGORY
   "What context makes our value obvious?"
```

### Positioning Statement Template

```
For [target customer who cares]
Who [statement of need/opportunity]
[Product name] is a [market category]
That [key benefit - the "so what"]
Unlike [competitive alternatives]
We [key differentiation]
```

### Common Traps

- **Phantom Competitors**: Listing companies you don't actually lose to
- **Too Broad**: "Anyone in X space" isn't actionable
- **New Category Assumption**: 90% of successful IPOs positioned in existing markets

## Phase 4: Validation Signals

### You've Found Your ICP When:

**Quantitative:**
- Higher conversion rates across all channels
- Better activation and retention than non-ICP
- Shorter sales cycles
- Higher win rates

**Qualitative:**
- Visible excitement in demos
- Specific, actionable feedback
- Spontaneously invite colleagues
- Ask about pricing unprompted
- Refer others

### Signs Your ICP is WRONG:

- "Interesting", "cool", "clever" feedback
- Use for unintended purposes
- Say they wouldn't pay
- Refer to junior non-decision makers
- Long cycles with "let me think about it"
