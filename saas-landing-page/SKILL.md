# SaaS Landing Page & Messaging

A complete framework for homepage copy, positioning, and messaging that converts visitors into users. Based on frameworks from MKT1, Growth Unhinged, and MRR Unlocked.

## The Core Problem

Most startup homepages fail because they answer the wrong questions. Visitors need to know:
1. **What does this do?** (Clarity)
2. **Is this for me?** (Relevance)
3. **Why this vs. alternatives?** (Differentiation)
4. **Can I trust them?** (Credibility)
5. **What do I do next?** (CTA)

If your homepage doesn't answer all 5 in under 10 seconds, you're losing users.

## Part 1: Positioning Framework (Do This First)

Before writing copy, answer these:

### The Positioning Statement

```
For [target customer]
who [have this problem / want this outcome],
[Product Name] is a [category]
that [key benefit / differentiator].
Unlike [main alternative],
we [what makes you different].
```

**Example:**
```
For marketing agencies
who struggle to keep clients updated on campaign performance,
ReportFlow is a client reporting tool
that auto-generates branded reports in minutes.
Unlike building reports manually in Google Slides,
we pull data from 20+ ad platforms automatically.
```

### 3 Positioning Archetypes

Choose one:
- **Category King:** You created or dominate a new category ("The only [X] for [Y]")
- **Best in Class:** You're the best at one specific thing ("The fastest / cheapest / simplest [X]")
- **Niche Focus:** You serve a specific customer better than generalists ("Built for [specific audience]")

## Part 2: Homepage Structure

### The Winning Homepage Formula

```
1. NAV BAR
   Logo | Features | Pricing | [CTA Button]

2. HERO (Above the fold — most critical)
   - Headline: What you do + for whom
   - Subheadline: How you do it + key benefit
   - CTA: Primary action
   - Social proof snippet: X users / X reviews / notable logos

3. SOCIAL PROOF BAR
   Logos of recognizable customers OR media mentions

4. PROBLEM SECTION
   "If you've ever [pain]..." — make them feel seen

5. SOLUTION SECTION
   How your product solves it (3-column feature/benefit layout)

6. PRODUCT DEMO
   Screenshot, GIF, or video walkthrough

7. FEATURE DETAILS
   Alternating text + image blocks for each key feature

8. SOCIAL PROOF (FULL)
   Testimonials with photos, names, companies

9. PRICING PREVIEW
   (Optional) Or "Starting at $X/mo"

10. FINAL CTA
    Repeat primary CTA + address last objection
```

## Part 3: Hero Section Templates

The hero is your most important copy. Test multiple variants.

### Template 1: Outcome-Led

```
Headline: [Achieve outcome] without [painful thing]
Subheadline: [Product] helps [audience] [do specific thing] so you can [benefit].
CTA: [Start for free / Get started / Try free for 14 days]
```

**Example:**
```
Headline: Send better client reports without the manual work
Subheadline: ReportFlow connects to your ad platforms and builds branded reports automatically — so you can focus on strategy, not spreadsheets.
CTA: Start free trial
```

---

### Template 2: Problem-Led

```
Headline: Tired of [specific painful thing]?
Subheadline: [Product] [solves it] by [mechanism]. [Social proof].
CTA: [Action]
```

---

### Template 3: Category-Led

```
Headline: The [adjective] [category] for [audience]
Subheadline: [What it does + key outcome]. Trusted by [X] [users/teams].
CTA: [Action]
```

---

### Template 4: Specificity-Led (most underused, highest converting)

```
Headline: [Specific outcome with number]
Subheadline: [How + for whom]
CTA: [Action]
```

**Example:**
```
Headline: Cut client reporting time from 4 hours to 20 minutes
Subheadline: ReportFlow auto-builds white-label reports from Google Ads, Meta, and LinkedIn data. For agencies managing 10+ clients.
CTA: See it in action
```

| Formula | Example |
|---------|---------|

## Part 5: Copy Principles

### Clarity Over Cleverness

```
❌ "Unlock the power of data-driven insights for your team"
✅ "See which campaigns are working — updated daily"
```

### Features → Benefits → Outcomes

```
Feature: 20+ integrations
↓
Benefit: Pulls data automatically
↓
Outcome: No more copy-pasting from dashboards
```

Always write at the **Outcome** level, not Feature level.

### Specificity Builds Trust

```
❌ "Saves you time"
✅ "Saves 3+ hours per client per month"

❌ "Trusted by thousands"
✅ "Trusted by 1,400+ marketing agencies"

❌ "Easy to use"
✅ "Set up in 15 minutes, no coding required"
```

### The 5-Second Test

Someone should be able to look at your homepage for 5 seconds and answer:
- What is this?
- Who is it for?
- What do I do next?

If they can't → rewrite the hero.

## Part 6: CTA Copy

### What Works

```
✅ Start free trial
✅ Try [Product] free
✅ Get started — it's free
✅ See [Product] in action (for demo-first products)
✅ Start building for free
✅ Join [X] teams already using [Product]
```

### What Doesn't Work

```
❌ Get started (vague)
❌ Learn more (weak)
❌ Book a demo (high friction for self-serve)
❌ Subscribe (sounds like newsletter)
❌ Submit (impersonal)
```

### Reduce Friction Under CTA

Add reassurance directly below the button:
```
No credit card required · Cancel anytime · Free for up to X users
```

## Part 7: Social Proof Copy

### Testimonial Formula

A great testimonial answers:
```
[Specific outcome] + [Timeframe] + [Context about them]
```

**Example:**
```
"We cut client reporting time by 70% in the first week.
As an agency managing 25 clients, this was a game-changer."
— Sarah K., Founder @ Growth Lab Agency
```

### Placement Strategy

| Type | Where |
|------|-------|
| Logos bar | Below hero |
| Star rating + count | Hero or near CTA |
| Short quotes | Feature sections |
| Long testimonials | Dedicated section or pricing page |
| Case study link | Near primary CTA |

## Part 8: Pricing Page Essentials

### DNA of a Great Pricing Page (Elena Verna framework)

1. **Clear tier names** — use role/outcome names, not arbitrary labels
   - ❌ Starter / Pro / Enterprise
   - ✅ Solo / Team / Agency (audience-based)
   - ✅ Launch / Scale / Enterprise (outcome-based)

2. **Highlight the recommended plan** — with "Most Popular" badge

3. **Feature comparison** — anchor the value of the higher tier

4. **Toggle (monthly/annual)** — show annual savings clearly

5. **FAQ section** — answer top 5 pricing objections

6. **Social proof on pricing page** — reduces buyer anxiety at decision point

7. **Money-back guarantee** — reduces risk

### Pricing Page FAQ Template

```
Q: Can I change plans later?
A: Yes, upgrade or downgrade anytime.

Q: What happens after the free trial?
A: [Explain clearly — don't hide it]

Q: Do you offer refunds?
A: [Your policy]

Q: Is there a free plan?
A: [Yes/No + details]

Q: Do you offer discounts for startups/nonprofits?
A: [Yes/No + how to apply]
```

## Messaging Audit Checklist

Run this on any existing homepage:

- [ ] Can a stranger understand what you do in 5 seconds?
- [ ] Is the target audience mentioned explicitly?
- [ ] Is there at least one specific outcome with a number?
- [ ] Is the primary CTA visible without scrolling?
- [ ] Are there trust signals above the fold?
- [ ] Does the subheadline expand on the headline (not repeat it)?
- [ ] Is the hero free of jargon and acronyms?
- [ ] Are testimonials specific (name, company, outcome)?
- [ ] Does the pricing page answer "what happens after trial"?
- [ ] Is there a FAQ that addresses the top 3 objections?

## Quick Reference

| Element | Goal | Max Length |
|---------|------|-----------|
| Headline | Clarity + hook | 8-12 words |
| Subheadline | Expand + benefit | 20-30 words |
| Feature description | Benefit, not feature | 1-2 sentences |
| Testimonial | Specific outcome | 2-3 sentences |
| CTA button | Action + value | 3-5 words |

**Golden Rule:** Every line of copy should either clarify what you do, who it's for, or why they should trust you. Delete everything else.
