# Idea Validator

A systematic framework for validating business ideas before building products, synthesized from the 7-Day Validation Framework (Maja Voje) and hypothesis-driven validation methodology (MRR Unlocked).

## Core Principle

> "People do > People say"

The most reliable validation is commitment (money, time, reputation), not opinions.

---

## The 7-Day Validation Framework

### Day 1: Vision & Market Insights

**SWOT Analysis:**
- **Strengths**: What unique advantage do you have?
- **Weaknesses**: What skills/gaps do you lack?
- **Opportunities**: What market trends favor this?
- **Threats**: What could kill this business?

**Key Questions:**
- What problem have you solved before that could scale?
- What's your unique insight about this market?
- Why you? Why now?

---

### Day 2: Minimum Viable Segment (ECP)

**ECP Selection Criteria (ALL must be met):**

| Criterion | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| Burning Pain | Do they need this solved NOW? |
| Willingness to Pay | Can they afford it? Will they pay? |
| Your Proximity | Can you realistically reach them? |
| Relevant Case Studies | Will their success help reach ICP? |

**Market-Problem Map:**
```
Persona: [Name your ECP]

PROBLEMS THEY FACE
├── Problem 1: [pain point]
│   ├── Current solution: [how they cope]
│   └── Cost: [time/money lost]
├── Problem 2: [pain point]
└── Problem 3: [pain point]

JOBS TO BE DONE
├── Functional: [what to accomplish]
├── Emotional: [how to feel]
└── Social: [how to be perceived]
```

---

### Day 3: Value Proposition

**Selection Criteria for Use Case:**
- Highly tactical → Immediate, concrete value
- Low time to value → Seconds to understand
- Frequently requested → What people ask for most

**Value Proposition Template:**
```
CURRENT STATE (Pain)
- What frustrates them today?
- What's broken?

DESIRED FUTURE STATE (Gain)
- What outcome do they want?
- What would success look like?

YOUR SOLUTION (Bridge)
- How do you get them from current to desired?

UNIQUE VALUE PROPOSITION
One sentence: [Clear, specific benefit]
```

---

### Day 4: Minimum Viable Positioning

**Competitive Alternatives Scoring:**

List alternatives (what customers would do if you didn't exist):
1. Hire a consultant
2. Use templates
3. Do nothing / status quo
4. Build it themselves

Rate each 1-5 on criteria that matter to ECP:
- Time to value
- Cost
- Quality
- Expertise required
- Customization

**Your differentiation = where you score 4-5 and alternatives score 1-2**

---

### Day 5: Select Validation Method

**Validation Methods (By Confidence Level):**

| Level | Methods | Reliability |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| LOW | Google Trends, market reports, competitor reviews | Research only |
| MEDIUM | Surveys, interviews, landing page | People saying |
| HIGH | Waitlist, pre-orders, LOI, actual sales | People doing |

**Prefer HIGH confidence methods. Money changing hands is the gold standard.**

---

### Day 6: Experiment Design

**MVP Types:**

| Type | Description | Best For |
|------|-------------|----------|
| Concierge | Do it manually | Services, complex workflows |
| Landing Page | Describe product, capture interest | Testing demand |
| Wizard of Oz | Front looks automated, back is manual | AI products |
| Pre-sales | Sell before building | B2B, high-ticket |
| Crowdfunding | Kickstarter, Product Hunt | Consumer products |

**Sample Size Targets:**
- Minimum: 30 serious testers
- Good: 100 engaged users
- Safe: 300+ users

**Define Success Metrics BEFORE launching:**
- 100 B2B waitlist signups in 2 weeks
- 20% of interviewees sign LOI
- 5% visitor-to-signup conversion
- 10 pre-orders at $99

---

### Day 7: Launch & Analysis

**Interview Questions (The Mom Test Style):**

✅ **Good questions:**
1. "What are the 3 most annoying problems you face with [category]?"
2. "How are you currently solving [specific problem]?"
3. "What's most frustrating about [current solution]?"
4. "Why haven't you already changed this?"
5. "Tell me about the last time you [did related activity]"

❌ **Bad questions (false positives):**
- "Do you think this is a good idea?"
- "Would you use this?"
- "How much would you pay?"

**Signals of Product-Market Fit:**
- Users demand it when offline
- Users offer to pay unprompted
- Users refer others
- Users give detailed feedback

## Hypothesis Validation Framework

### Step 1: List Core Assumptions (up to 25)

Assumptions about:
- Customer (who they are, what they need)
- Problem (how painful, current solutions)
- Solution (whether your approach works)
- Market (size, growth, competition)
- Business (pricing, distribution)

### Step 2: Score and Prioritize

```
For each hypothesis:
- Impact (1-10): How important to success?
- Uncertainty (1-10): How unsure are you?

Score = Impact × Uncertainty

Validate highest scores FIRST.
```

### Step 3: Write Testable Hypotheses

```
IF [action/cause]
THEN [measurable outcome]
WITHIN [timeframe]

Example:
"If I send 100 cold emails to [ICP], then 10% will book a call within 1 week"
```

## Quick Reference

| Situation | Do This | Don't |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| Just have idea | Talk to 10 customers | Build anything |
| Problem validated | Landing page + waitlist | Build full product |
| Some interest | Pre-sell at discount | Add features |
| Pre-sold | Build minimum | Over-engineer |
| Not getting traction | Talk to more customers | Pivot randomly |

**Golden Rule:** Money changing hands > everything else.
